Who I am

About

📍 Zagreb, Croatia 🇮🇱 Israeli 👨‍👧‍👦 Father of Adam & Ella 10 yrs experience 🤝 Available for projects

I'm Gal Tidhar — an Israeli software engineer who moved to Zagreb, married a Zagorka, and never left. Father of Adam and Ella. For over a decade I've built systems that run at scale, at companies from Siemens to Shopify to early-stage startups.

Today I work with businesses who want to operate smarter — whether that means automating the tasks that eat your Mondays, building a website that actually brings in customers, or creating a system built around how your business really works.

ZaZet Solutions is named after me. Zagorski Zet — a Croatian term for the foreigner who married into a Zagorje family. I brought the engineering. Croatia gave me the context, the perspective, and the two best kids in the world.

In Zagorje, ordering a gemišt is not just choosing a drink. You hold up two fingers. One vertical — the wine, the dominant force. One horizontal — the water, just enough to make it work. That ratio, prst prst, is the local name for the strong version: 8 parts wine, 2 parts water. Not the tourist pour. The real one. What's gemišt? →

Look at the ZaZet logo. Blue at the top — precise, technical, the 20%. Gold fills the rest — warm, decisive, the impact. That is the ratio of what I bring to your project.
